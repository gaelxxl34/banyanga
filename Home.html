BANYANGA is a Semi-Bantu tribe located in walikale one of the richest territory in congo with gold, cobalt, iranium and very many others resources

Banyanga is the biggest tribe in the territory of walikale located in the province of North-kivu in the Democratic Republic of Congo. It is a tribe rich in culture with more exotic traditions and an incomparable patriotism in terms of sincere love is deep towards our beautiful country which is the DRC. to know more about the banyanga, the best way is to discover their natural habitat which is WALIKALE one of the richest territory in terms of mining resources and underground treasures of all kinds

WALIKALE DR-CONGO

Walikale is a town in the North Kivu Province of the Democratic Republic of the Congo (DRC). It is the administrative center of the Walikale Territory. Walikale is home to the largest tin deposits in the Congo and to some very large gold mines, as well. In particular, the Bisie mine is supposed to account for somewhere between 50 and 80% of t in exports from North Kivu. The control of Bisie mine is a main source of contention within the Congolese army, as it provides for upwards of $100,000 a month in taxes for local soldiers. This does not include the individual pits that some commanders control and or the kickbacks they get from trading houses.

Our Culture

All Banyanga are naturally proud and respect their culture. the Banyanga speak a language c alled kinyinga and the minimum to know in this language is 'buni' which means 'hello' and 'nie miasi' which means 'how are you' this minimum makes it possible to fraternize with the banyanga who are moreover a generous and very hospitable people

Our Music

The Banyanga celebrate the joy and happiness of living by having a unique, folkloric, and original style of music that makes them even more special. these traditional dances are often done while dressing in rafia to respect the culture

Our Origin

Since the 1890s the Banyanga have occupied the eastern part of the Democratic Republic of the Congo, they have suffered colonization but fought fiercely and participated in the independence of the country, which makes them real pure-blooded Congolese.
